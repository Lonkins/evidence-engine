# Character File — Nora Ashton

**Full name:** Nora Eleanor Ashton  
**Date of birth:** 22 February 1973  
**Age:** 52  
**Address:** The Limes, Long Melford, Suffolk CO10  
**Occupation:** Private investor, art collector  
**Net worth (estimated):** £8–12m (trade sources)  

---

## Background

Nora Ashton is a private collector who built her collection primarily through acquisitions of Dutch and Flemish 17th-century works. She has purchased six works from Holbrooke Gallery since 2017, with a total acquisition value of approximately £940,000. Her most recent acquisition through the gallery (February 2024) was a Jan Miense Molenaer genre scene at £165,000.

She is not known to have any professional relationship with Helena Voss or Felix Drummond beyond their mutual gallery connection.

---

## Reason for Presence in Camden on 14 October 2025

Nora Ashton visited Camden on the afternoon of 14 October for a separate matter: a private viewing at the Delancey Gallery (12 Delancey Street), approximately 600 metres from Holbrooke Gallery. She attended the viewing between 16:30 and 18:15 per the Delancey Gallery's visitor record.

She states that she did not visit Holbrooke Gallery that evening and did not enter the premises. She was, however, in the general area.

---

## CCTV Record

External CCTV footage from a neighbouring business on Westbourne Terrace shows an individual consistent with Nora Ashton's description (height, clothing, distinctive red coat) on the pavement outside Holbrooke Gallery at approximately 20:40. The footage shows the individual pausing briefly, looking at the gallery frontage, and continuing to walk east.

Nora has not been asked directly about this CCTV footage in initial questioning.

---

## Relationship with Victor Holt

Nora Ashton and Victor Holt maintained a cordial collector–dealer relationship. An email exchange in September 2025 shows Nora enquiring about the de Heem acquisition (HG-2025-004) with a view to purchasing. Victor replied that the work was under a provisional commitment to another buyer and was "not currently available." Nora's reply expressed disappointment.

A subsequent email from Nora on 3 October 2025 asks: "Is the provenance issue I've heard whispers about in the trade going to affect the sale?" Victor did not reply to this email.

---

## Demeanour (DI Sullivan's initial observation)

Nora Ashton was contacted by telephone on 15 October. She was cooperative and provided a statement the same afternoon. She did not volunteer information about the CCTV or her presence near the gallery at 20:40. She expressed surprise and sadness at Victor's death and described him as "a man of unusual integrity in a trade that often lacks it."
