# Character File — Helena Voss

**Full name:** Helena Marie Voss  
**Date of birth:** 12 July 1984  
**Age:** 41  
**Address:** 18A Belsize Avenue, Hampstead, London NW3  
**Occupation:** Head Curator, Holbrooke Gallery  
**Employment start:** September 2018  

---

## Background

Helena Voss holds a doctorate in Northern European art history from the Courtauld Institute of Art (2011) and a postgraduate diploma in cultural property law (2013). Before joining Holbrooke Gallery she was assistant curator at the Rijksmuseum Acquisition Research Unit in Amsterdam for three years, where she specialised in provenance tracing for works with gaps in ownership history between 1933 and 1945.

She was recruited by Victor Holt personally following a paper she presented at the Tefaf Maastricht symposium on attribution methodology.

---

## Responsibilities at Holbrooke Gallery

- Acquisition research and provenance due diligence for prospective purchases
- Loan and exhibition coordination
- Vendor relationship management
- Day-to-day gallery operations in the owner's absence
- Gallery budget management for acquisitions (delegated authority up to £50,000; above that requires Victor's counter-signature)

---

## Acquisition: de Heem (Spring 2025)

In March 2025, Helena proposed the acquisition of a still-life painting attributed to Jan Davidszoon de Heem from a private vendor introduced through a Zurich intermediary. The asking price was €340,000. Helena prepared and presented the provenance documentation, which included a certificate of authenticity issued by the Flemish Art Research Institute (FARI) and export licences from the Belgian authorities.

Victor approved the acquisition in April 2025. The work was accessioned as HG-2025-004. A buyer had provisionally committed at £285,000; the transaction was pending final agreement at the time of Victor's death.

## Demeanour (DI Sullivan's initial observation)

Helena Voss presented as composed and articulate during initial questioning. She was the first person to contact the gallery's solicitors following Victor's death. She asked on two occasions whether she was considered a suspect.
