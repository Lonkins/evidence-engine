# Post-Mortem Examination Summary

**Report reference:** PM-HGA-2025-1014  
**Pathologist:** Dr Sylvia Okafor, HM Coroner's Pathologist  
**Examination date:** 15 October 2025  
**Report date:** 16 October 2025 (preliminary summary; full report to follow)  

---

## Cause of Death

**1a:** Subdural haematoma with transtentorial herniation  
**1b:** Blunt force trauma to the posterior cranium  

Death resulted from a single blow to the posterior cranium with a blunt implement. The injury profile is consistent with the ceramic lamp recovered from the scene (exhibit HGA-002). The force required was within the capacity of an adult assailant of average or greater strength.

---

## Time of Death

**Estimated range:** 20:30 to 21:15 on 14 October 2025.

This estimate is based on:
- Core body temperature at examination: 30.2°C (ambient room temperature: 19°C)
- Degree of rigor mortis
- Stomach contents and digestion state (Victor Holt consumed a light meal; estimated 4–5 hours prior to death)
- Lividity pattern

The range cannot be narrowed further without additional data. The midpoint of the range is approximately 20:52.

---

## Additional Findings

- No defensive wounds on the hands or forearms. The blow appears to have been struck from behind or while the victim was unaware. The seated posture in the office chair is consistent with the injury location.
- Blood alcohol: 0.03% (trace; well below any legal threshold).
- Toxicology panel: no prescribed or controlled substances detected.
- General health: the victim was in good health for his age; no pre-existing condition that would have contributed to death.

---

## Remarks

The absence of defensive wounds and the single-blow nature of the injury suggest the assailant had the victim's trust or was able to approach him from behind without arousing alarm. This is consistent with the victim's office being accessed by someone familiar with both the access system and the victim's routine.
