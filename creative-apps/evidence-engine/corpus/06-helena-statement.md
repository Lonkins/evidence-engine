# Witness Statement — Helena Voss

**Statement reference:** HGA-2025-1014-WS-01  
**Name:** Helena Marie Voss  
**Date of statement:** 15 October 2025, 10:30  
**Taken by:** Detective Constable Priya Mehta  
**Location:** Camden CID, Agar Street  

---

## Statement

I arrived at Holbrooke Gallery at my usual time of approximately nine in the morning on Tuesday the fourteenth. I spent the morning and early afternoon on correspondence and working on the catalogue entry for the de Heem acquisition. Victor arrived at around eleven. He seemed distracted, which was not unusual for him during periods when he was preparing for a significant sale.

At around five o'clock I oversaw the public closing of the gallery. Mr Drummond — Felix — arrived shortly afterwards for his appraisal appointment. I had made the arrangements for his visit and showed him around the collection. Victor was present initially but withdrew to his office after approximately half an hour. I remained with Felix until he had finished the physical assessment, which was at perhaps a quarter to seven in the evening.

Victor asked to speak with me privately after Felix had settled in the reading room to complete his notes. We spoke in his office for approximately fifteen minutes. The conversation was about gallery administration. Nothing significant was discussed.

I then returned to the main gallery to inform Felix that I was leaving. He confirmed he would let himself out. I left my keys at the front desk — one set for the gallery, which I locked from the outside, and the spare set, which I left with Felix as he had requested an additional period to complete his paperwork.

I locked the gallery at approximately a quarter to eight in the evening and handed the door key to Mr Drummond, as he had requested an additional thirty minutes to complete his paperwork. I then walked to the Underground.

I was at home in Belsize Avenue by approximately eight-fifteen. I do not recall receiving any telephone calls that evening. I learned of Victor's death when Detective Constable Mehta telephoned me at seven forty-five the following morning.

I am not aware of any reason why Victor would have remained at the gallery after I left. He appeared his usual self when we spoke. I did not hear anything that concerned me during the evening.
