# Case Overview — The Holbrooke Gallery Affair

**Case reference:** HGA-2025-1014  
**Incident type:** Suspicious death (possible homicide)  
**Date of incident:** Tuesday, 14 October 2025  
**Location:** Holbrooke Gallery, 27 Westbourne Terrace, Camden, London  
**Reporting officer:** Detective Inspector Ruth Sullivan, Camden CID  

---

## Incident Summary

At 21:42 on Tuesday 14 October 2025, a 999 call was placed by Felix Drummond (34), an independent insurance assessor. Mr Drummond reported discovering the body of Victor Holt (58), owner of Holbrooke Gallery, in the gallery's private office. Mr Drummond stated he had returned to the gallery after a dinner appointment to collect paperwork he had left behind, and found the main door unlocked and Mr Holt unresponsive at his desk.

Responding officers attended at 21:58 and confirmed the scene. Detective Inspector Ruth Sullivan took charge of the investigation at 22:30.

## Physical Scene

The victim was found seated at his desk in the gallery's private office. The office door was ajar. Signs of a struggle were limited but present: a ceramic desk lamp found on the floor, documents disturbed across the desk surface, a desk drawer open. Cause of death: blunt force trauma to the posterior cranium.

No murder weapon recovered at scene.

The main gallery door was found unlocked. The victim's personal mobile phone was recovered on the desk; no sign of a burglary.

## Persons of Interest

All three individuals present at the gallery that evening — Helena Voss, Felix Drummond, and Nora Ashton — have been asked to provide statements. All have agreed to cooperate.
