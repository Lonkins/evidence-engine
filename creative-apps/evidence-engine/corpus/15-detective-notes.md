## Summary of Evidence Position (as at 16 October 2025)

Three individuals were present at or near Holbrooke Gallery on the evening of 14 October 2025. I will address each in turn.

---

### Felix Drummond

Felix Drummond's account is supported by the security log (exit at 19:48) and his Oyster card (Camden Town tap-in at 19:52). He was seated at dinner in Islington from 20:35 onward, confirmed by restaurant receipt and Oyster tap-out at Angel at 20:11. The journey time from Holbrooke Gallery to Angel is 12–18 minutes by Underground.

Given the estimated time of death (20:30–21:15), Felix Drummond was demonstrably not at the gallery during the murder window. He cannot have committed this crime.

---

### Nora Ashton

Nora Ashton's CCTV appearance near the gallery at 20:40 is notable. She did not volunteer this information. However, the footage shows her on the pavement; there is no footage of her entering the building. Her Oyster tap-in at Camden Town at 21:20 and the confirmed train booking at 21:43 are consistent with her statement.

She had a reason to be in the area. She had a provenance question outstanding with the gallery. She may have been considering calling in unannounced. None of this is evidence of her presence inside the building.

The badge access log, which is the authoritative record of entry and exit via the card reader, shows no access events attributable to Nora Ashton. She does not have an access card to the gallery.

I do not consider Nora Ashton the primary suspect at this stage, though I intend to re-interview her about the 20:40 CCTV sighting.

---

### Helena Voss

Helena Voss's statement asserts she left the gallery at approximately 19:45. This is directly contradicted by the electronic access log, which records her badge exiting the main door at 20:47:33 — approximately one hour later, and within the time of death window.

Furthermore:
- Her mobile phone records show she called Victor at 20:11 and 20:18 while — per the badge log — she was physically inside the gallery. If she had left at 19:45 as she claims, those calls would have been made from outside.
- The unsent email recovered from Victor's laptop establishes that he intended to confront Helena that evening about what he believed was a fraudulent provenance — a fraud that, if proved, would expose her to criminal prosecution and destroy her career.
- Felix Drummond's preliminary assessment of the de Heem painting on the evening in question added a further corroborating voice raising concerns about authenticity — this information was communicated to Helena.
- The fibres recovered from the victim's right shoulder are consistent with a garment owned by Helena Voss.

---

## Assessment

The evidence currently points strongly to Helena Voss as the person responsible for Victor Holt's death. The motive is clear: Victor had discovered the fraud, was planning to report it, and confronted her that evening. The means: the ceramic desk lamp, present in the office, accessible. The opportunity: she was physically present in the gallery between approximately 19:00 and 20:47, within the time of death window, alone with the victim after Felix Drummond departed at 19:48.

The primary unresolved question is the unattributed SMS to Helena at 17:51 ("Is it done?") and her call to an unknown number at 20:51 — four minutes after her badge exit. This may indicate a co-conspirator or a separate thread requiring further investigation.

---

## Next Steps

- Re-interview Helena Voss under caution.
- Obtain records for the unattributed phone numbers.
- Commission fibre analysis comparing grey-green jacket fibres.
- Pursue FARI independently to confirm certificate forgery.
- Obtain Helena Voss's financial records for the twelve months prior to the acquisition.
