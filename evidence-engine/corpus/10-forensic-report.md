# Forensic Scene Examination Report

**Report reference:** FSE-HGA-2025-1014  
**Examiner:** Senior Crime Scene Investigator Damien Cross, Metropolitan Police Forensic Services  
**Scene:** Holbrooke Gallery private office and main gallery space  
**Examination date:** 14–15 October 2025  
**Report date:** 17 October 2025  

---

## Scene Overview

The private office (approximately 18m²) is located at the rear of the gallery. It is accessed via a card-reader secured door from the main corridor. The room contains a large oak desk, two visitor chairs, bookshelves along the north and east walls, a small safe (locked, undisturbed), and a connecting door to a washroom.

The victim was found seated in the office chair, slumped forward over the desk. The office door was ajar (approximately 25 degrees open) when first attending officers arrived.

---

## Physical Evidence Summary

### Probable Assault Area

The evidence is consistent with an assault occurring at the desk area:

1. **Ceramic desk lamp (exhibit HGA-002):** Found on the office floor approximately 1.4 metres from the desk. The lamp base shows fracture damage consistent with impact with a hard surface. Blood trace matching the victim's blood type identified on the lamp base (DNA confirmation pending). No fingerprints recovered from the lamp base — wiped or gloved contact.

2. **Desk surface disturbance:** Papers and a desktop organiser overturned; ink pen lodged under the overturned organiser. Consistent with a brief physical confrontation or the victim falling forward.

3. **Blood evidence:** Haematic pooling on the desk surface at the posterior cranium impact zone. Minimal secondary splatter pattern consistent with a single impact event rather than repeated blows.

4. **Desk drawer (right-side middle, found open):** Contents disturbed; one item of correspondence removed (absence identified from folder index — exhibit HGA-003). No fingerprints recovered from the drawer interior.

### Main Gallery Area

No physical evidence of a struggle in the main gallery. All display cases locked and undisturbed. No items missing from display per gallery manager's initial inventory.

---

## Fingerprint and DNA Evidence

- **No foreign fingerprints recovered** from the lamp, desk surface, or immediate area of the victim. All identifiable fingerprints in the office match the victim or Helena Voss (as expected for the office they shared access to).
- **No foreign DNA recovered** from the office. This is consistent with an assailant wearing gloves or taking care to avoid leaving biological material.
- **Fibres (exhibit HGA-004):** Two fibres recovered from the victim's right shoulder. Forensic textile analysis confirms wool/cashmere blend, consistent with a women's high-quality jacket or coat. Colour: grey-green. No match to any garment taken from Felix Drummond's possession. A grey-green wool-cashmere jacket owned by Helena Voss has been identified; fibre comparison analysis is pending.

---

## Entry/Exit Assessment

The main entrance was found unlocked when Felix Drummond arrived at 21:44. No signs of forced entry anywhere in the premises. The assailant either had a key/access card or was admitted by the victim. The card access log is the authoritative record of electronic entry and exit; no card entries after Felix Drummond's exit at 19:48 until Felix's return at 21:44 (door held open/alarm triggered).
