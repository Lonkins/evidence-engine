# Victim Profile — Victor Holt

**Full name:** Victor James Holt  
**Date of birth:** 3 March 1967  
**Age at death:** 58  
**Address:** Flat 4, Pembridge Mansions, Notting Hill, London W11  
**Occupation:** Gallery owner, art dealer  
**Gallery founded:** 1998  

---

## Background

Victor Holt founded Holbrooke Gallery in Camden in 1998 following a career as a commercial dealer in 17th-century Dutch and Flemish masters. The gallery built a reputation for specialist Old Masters acquisitions and provenance research. In recent years it diversified into mid-century British abstraction.

Victor was widely known in the London art trade as meticulous about provenance — he had given evidence to the Arts Council's cultural property working group on three occasions and was described in trade journals as "uncompromisingly rigorous."

---

## Professional Relationships

- **Helena Voss** — employed as head curator since 2018. Victor publicly described her as "the best eye in the room." She managed day-to-day gallery operations and acquisitions research. Salary: £68,000 per annum.
- **Felix Drummond** — contracted irregularly for insurance appraisals. Known to Victor through the trade. No personal relationship noted.
- **Nora Ashton** — long-standing client. Purchased six works through the gallery between 2017–2024. Relationship described by staff as "professional and cordial."

---

## Financial Position

Gallery accounts for the twelve months ending March 2025 show turnover of £1.4m and a pre-tax profit of £187,000. No outstanding debts or creditor proceedings noted. Gallery premises held on a 25-year lease expiring 2043.

---

## Personal Life

Divorced 2016. No children. Next of kin: sister, Patricia Holt-Renner, Edinburgh. Estranged from spouse since divorce; no ongoing contact noted.

---

## Known Recent Concerns

Gallery staff reported that Victor had been "distracted and agitated" in the two weeks before his death, spending long periods in his office and making several unexplained calls to the gallery's solicitors. The nature of those concerns was not discussed with staff.

Staff also noted that a significant acquisition made in spring 2025 — attributed to Jan Davidszoon de Heem, acquired by Helena Voss on the gallery's behalf at €340,000 — had been the subject of a "quiet review" at Victor's instruction in early October 2025.
