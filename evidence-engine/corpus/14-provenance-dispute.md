## Email Draft (Verbatim)

**To:** [DS A Fenn, Metropolitan Police — Art & Antiques Unit]; [Croft & Keel Solicitors]  
**Subject:** Suspected fraudulent provenance — acquisition HG-2025-004 / FARI certificate ref FARI-2025-0387  
**Draft saved:** 14 October 2025, 16:32  

---

Andrew,

I am writing to report what I now believe to be a deliberate fraud perpetrated in connection with an acquisition made through my gallery in April of this year.

The work in question is a still-life painting submitted for acquisition by my head curator, Helena Voss, and attributed to Jan Davidszoon de Heem. The purchase price was €340,000 from a Zurich intermediary identified to me by Helena only as acting "on behalf of a private estate." The provenance documentation, prepared and presented by Helena, included a certificate of authenticity from the Flemish Art Research Institute (FARI) bearing reference FARI-2025-0387.

Following concerns raised by the prospective buyer's own advisers in early October, I commissioned an independent review of the FARI certificate. I received the result of that review this morning. FARI has confirmed that no certificate bearing the reference FARI-2025-0387 was issued by them, and that the document presented to me is a forgery.

I have also been unable to independently verify the Belgian export licence submitted as part of the provenance bundle.

My solicitors have advised me that I am required to report this to you immediately, and I am doing so. I intend to confront Helena this evening and ask for her account before filing a formal complaint. I am aware this may not be the most procedurally correct course of action and I accept that guidance.

The acquisition funds of €340,000 were disbursed from gallery account [REDACTED] on 18 April 2025. The receiving account details are held by my solicitors and will be provided to you on request.

I will call you tomorrow morning.

Victor

---

**Exhibit note (DC Mehta):** This draft was recovered from a temporary documents folder and was not sent. Victor Holt was killed the same evening the draft was saved, before it could be sent. The reference to confronting Helena "this evening" is consistent with the timeline and with the private conversation between Victor and Helena recorded in both parties' statements as occurring at approximately 19:00–19:15 that evening.
