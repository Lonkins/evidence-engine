## Statement

I was in Camden on Tuesday the fourteenth of October for a private viewing at the Delancey Gallery on Delancey Street. I have attended viewings there on perhaps eight or nine occasions over the past three years. I can confirm the Delancey Gallery will have a record of my visit.

I arrived in the area by train in the early afternoon and attended the viewing from approximately half past four until just after six in the evening. After the viewing I walked for a while — I often do this when I am in London, as I find it preferable to taking the Underground in the early evening when it is crowded.

I walked in the general direction of Holbrooke Gallery at some point during my walk. I know the street well from previous visits. I did not enter the gallery or the building. I did not attempt to do so. The gallery appeared to be closed — the lights were on in the back, but the blinds in the front window were down.

I did not contact Victor that evening and I did not attempt to do so. Our most recent email exchange was in October, as I had heard trade gossip about a provenance question on a recent acquisition. Victor did not reply to my last message and I had not followed up.

I took the Northern Line from Camden Town to King's Cross at approximately twenty past nine and caught the 21:43 to Cambridge, which I can confirm with my rail ticket. I was in Long Melford by midnight.

I was very sorry to hear about Victor's death. He was a meticulous and honourable dealer. I cannot imagine why anyone would wish to harm him.
