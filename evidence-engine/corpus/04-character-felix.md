# Character File — Felix Drummond

**Full name:** Felix John Drummond  
**Date of birth:** 8 November 1991  
**Age:** 34  
**Address:** 42 Goswell Road, Clerkenwell, London EC1  
**Occupation:** Independent insurance assessor, fine art and antiques  
**Trading as:** Drummond Fine Art Assessment Ltd (incorporated 2019)  

---

## Background

Felix Drummond qualified as a chartered surveyor in 2016 before transitioning to specialist fine art assessment following two years at Christie's valuation services. He established his own practice in 2019 and works primarily with Lloyd's syndicates and mid-market insurers requiring independent assessments for high-value works.

He has no prior criminal record and no adverse professional findings on record.

---

## Engagement at Holbrooke Gallery

Felix was engaged on 1 October 2025 by Holbrooke Gallery's insurer (Meridian Specialist Risks, Policy HG-45122) to conduct a periodic revaluation of the gallery's principal collection following the new acquisition (HG-2025-004). The engagement letter specified an after-hours visit on the evening of 14 October 2025, with a four-hour assessment window.

Victor Holt and Helena Voss were both present at commencement of the assessment at 17:30.

---

## Felix's Account of the Evening (Summary — see full statement)

Felix states he completed the physical assessment of the gallery's held works by approximately 18:45. He then spent approximately one hour preparing preliminary notes in the gallery's main reading room. He states that he left the gallery at approximately 19:45 in the company of Helena Voss, and that Helena locked the door behind them and handed him the key.

Following his departure he took the Northern Line to Islington and attended a dinner reservation at Foragers Restaurant, 14 Chapel Market, Islington, at 20:35.

At approximately 21:30 Felix realised he had left his assessment laptop at the gallery. He returned by taxi. On arriving he found the main door unlocked, entered, and discovered Victor Holt unresponsive in the private office. He immediately called 999.

---

## Dinner Alibi

Felix's dinner at Foragers Restaurant is confirmed by a card payment receipt timed at 22:14 (card present, chip-and-PIN, card ending 4471) for £87.50 (table for one). The restaurant is located at 14 Chapel Market, Islington — approximately 3.2 miles from Holbrooke Gallery. Journey time by taxi: 12–18 minutes under normal conditions.

His Oyster card records show a tap-in at Camden Town Underground at 19:52 and tap-out at Angel at 20:11.

---

## Demeanour (DI Sullivan's initial observation)

Felix Drummond appeared genuinely distressed at the scene and during initial questioning. He was cooperative and provided contact details for his solicitor proactively. He expressed concern that discovering the body would complicate his professional relationship with the gallery's insurer.
